# The three motifs + the pit library

Everything the board writes ladders back to one of these. They are the brand.

## The three motifs

- **small on purpose** — we are not the long tail. The bench is curated and small by design. Length is a feature, not a constraint.
- **real-time** — the bench is current today. Not last quarter's roster. The "available now" column is the wedge.
- **no agency in the middle** — direct line to the talent. We don't represent them, we route to them.

Every post hits one of these (often two, occasionally three). If a post hits none, it doesn't belong.

## The pit-in-stomach library

The shape: name a moment the reader has already lived. Resolution implicit.

- the deadline didn't move.
- you've been scrolling your contacts since 9am.
- no agency in the middle. just a list, and a chat.
- you opened upwork. closed upwork. opened upwork.
- monday demo. no landing copy. it's saturday night.
- "anyone know a good copywriter?" (heard at 11:47am, every week)
- the question isn't who's good. it's who's good and available right now.
- we keep meeting people who built whole companies because they couldn't find one good freelancer.
- you can do it yourself. it'll take the weekend.

New entries get added by the board over time. Each pit is a moment the buyer has already lived. Specificity wins.

## What is NOT a motif

Reach for the pit instead of any of these:

- "Talent marketplace" — generic, owned by Upwork/Fiverr/Toptal.
- "Curated network" — every competitor says this. Show, don't tell.
- "Find your match" — dating-app energy.
- "We've got you covered" — fake warmth.
- "On-demand talent" — corporate.
- "Vetted freelancers" — every competitor.
