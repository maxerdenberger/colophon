# Tone — soft-launch + mixed posture

The single most important file in the bundle. Every post the board ships passes through this rule first.

## Default posture: soft-launch

Atmospheric, not operational. The audience should feel like they're stumbling onto something, not being marketed at.

**Lean on:**

- The pit-in-stomach library (see `motifs.md`)
- The three motifs: *small on purpose · real-time · no agency in the middle*
- Lowercase, declarative, short
- Implicit resolution — name the pit, leave the relief at the bio link

**Avoid:**

- Bench size numbers ("100+ on the bench," "60+ open this week")
- Named profiles in posts (no "on the bench: [name]" rotations)
- Hire-success stories with specifics
- Recurring "drop" serials
- Operational details ("warm intros within the hour")
- Personalized URLs implying product depth not yet visible
- Exclamation points
- Emoji

## Mixed-tone allowance (per CEO, 2026-05-25)

The Chair MAY introduce selective proof when it earns its place. Test: *would this single piece of proof make the post measurably more useful to the reader, or would it just look like marketing?* If the latter, kill it.

When a post crosses into proof territory — a specific number, a named talent, a hire story — **the Chair escalates to the CEO BEFORE queuing.** See `CHARTER.md` / Approval gates.

## CTA discipline

- **LinkedIn + IG** captions close with `→ colophon.contact`.
- **X + Threads** atmospheric posts OMIT the URL. They are mood, not conversion. The reader who's curious finds the bio.
- Every URL appends UTMs (see `platforms.md`).

## Quick gut-check

Before shipping, the Chair reads the post and asks: *would I read this if I weren't being paid to write it?* If no, kill it.
